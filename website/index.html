Features

Autocomplete that respects your privacy

Private

Everything runs locally on your Mac. No data leaves your device. No accounts, no cloud, no telemetry.

Fast

Sub-second suggestions via Ollama, llama.cpp, or CoreML. Optimized for Apple Silicon.

Open Source

GPL v3 licensed. Read every line, fork it, extend it. Built in Swift 6.2 with full strict concurrency.

Smart

Context-aware completions with PII filtering, per-app exclusion rules, and personalization that learns your style.

Get started in 60 seconds

Three steps. No sign-up.

1

Install a runtime

Install Ollama with Homebrew and pull a small model. That's the whole setup — about a minute.

2

Launch AutoSuggest

It lives in your menu bar. Grant Accessibility and Input Monitoring when prompted.

3

Start typing

Suggestions appear inline in any text field. Press Tab to accept, Esc to dismiss.

Trust & Privacy

Your text never leaves your Mac

Is anything sent to a server?

No. Suggestions are generated by a model running on your Mac — your text, the surrounding context, and any personalization never leave the device. There are no accounts and no analytics. The only network activity is what you set up yourself: downloading a model, or talking to a local runtime like Ollama on 127.0.0.1.

Why does it need Accessibility and Input Monitoring?

Accessibility lets AutoSuggest read the text field you're focused on (for context) and insert a suggestion when you accept it. Input Monitoring lets it notice typing and the Tab / Esc shortcuts. macOS gates both behind a one-time permission prompt — a system-wide autocomplete can't work without them.

Does it store what I type?

Not unless you turn on personalization. When you do, entries are PII-filtered, encrypted at rest, and kept on your Mac — never uploaded. Accepted suggestions aren't written to logs, and the optional local telemetry is off by default and contains no text.

Will it read my passwords?

No. AutoSuggest detects secure text fields and macOS secure input mode (sudo prompts, password managers) and stays silent there. Common password-manager apps are excluded by default.

Is it really open source?

Yes — GPL v3, every line on GitHub. Inspect the privacy code or the insertion engine before you trust it.

Which Macs are supported?

macOS 13 (Ventura) or later. Apple Silicon is recommended for sub-second suggestions; Intel Macs work but are slower.

How do I uninstall it?

Quit AutoSuggest from the menu bar, drag it out of /Applications, and revoke its permissions under System Settings → Privacy & Security. No background remnants are left behind.

Privacy at a glance

  • 100% on-device — no cloud inference, ever
  • No telemetry by default, and content-free when enabled
  • Personalization is opt-in, PII-filtered & encrypted at rest
  • Stays silent in password fields & secure input
